Hello, I am a 2nd year PhD student in the Psychology and Neuroscience department at Duke University who loves thinking about cognitive control! Currently, my work focuses on understanding the behavioral and neural underpinnings of attention switching through the use of both behavioral tasks and fMRI. I'm passionate about teaching and mentorship, having taught various courses including cognitive neuroscience, research methods, and python programming for undergraduates as either a TA or instructor. I also mentor several undergraduate students, focusing on promoting accessibility and inclusivity in scientific research. Music is also a major part of my life, and I enjoy film photography and nature!
